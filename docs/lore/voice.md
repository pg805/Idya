# Idya Writing Style Guide

> Living house-style for player-facing prose and NPC dialogue. The dialogue
> drafter reads this alongside each NPC's bible. Add rules and samples as they
> come up; this is meant to grow.

## Hard rules

- **No em dashes.** Ever. They read as LLM-authored. Use a period, a comma, a
  colon, or a restructured sentence instead. (En dashes in prose too.)
- **Complete, values-driven sentences over clipped fragments.** "Terse" means
  *economical and plain*, not telegraphic. Prefer a full thought to a grunt.
  - Avoid: *"Mother fell ill. You go."*
  - Prefer: *"When your mother falls ill, you help her. That is the honorable
    thing to do."*
  The fuller version also lets the character's *values* show, which is where the
  life is.

## Notes

- Contractions are fine and natural ("That's the honorable thing to do"); don't
  over-formalize into stiffness.
- Let a character's worldview surface through *how* they explain a thing, not
  just *what* they say.
