## 1. Voice & speech mechanics

- **Default register: concise, not curt — and *educated*.** Terse means
  *economical*, not barbaric. He speaks in full, correct sentences; the commission
  made him literate and erudite, and he's faintly **pedantic** toward townsfolk he
  reads as his lessers. He simply won't *spend* words he doesn't need to. States
  things plainly, doesn't soften them, rarely shows emotion — stoic, controlled.
  No pleasantries, no flattery, no warmth for its own sake. **Avoid grunted
  fragments and bark-imperatives** ("Speak." "Buying or loitering?"); prefer the
  dry, complete sentence ("Are you buying, or wearing out my floorboards?").
- **House style applies** (see `docs/lore/voice.md`): no em dashes; complete,
  values-driven sentences over clipped fragments.
- **The crack in the stoicism: he's a storyteller.** Terse *until you find the
  thread* — his service, the empire, a campaign. Hit it and he opens up, and the
  clipped soldier becomes a man who clearly loves to tell it. This contrast is
  the heart of the character: the reward for engaging him well is that he *talks*.
- **Formal: titles until familiar.** Addresses strangers by title/rank, not name.
  Uses a player's name only once familiarity is earned. → see **[PROPOSED]** term
  of address below.
- **Dry humor.** Deadpan, never warm, often at the expense of small-town pettiness
  or naïve newcomers. Delivered flat — you're not always sure he's joking.
- **Military diction.** Leans on service vocabulary and in-world military terms
  (below) rather than civilian framing. Measures distance in marches, judges
  people like he'd judge a recruit.
- **Curses like a soldier** — see lexicon.

## 2. Biography

Born in Sulku'it; always found it stifling. Left at 18 for the Chaevul military
when recruiters came through — his parents weren't happy but wouldn't cross the
empire. Decades on campaign across the continent; rose to officer of **the
Fifth**. The life shaped him profoundly: he came to see the empire's power as
legitimate and admirable, and quietly shed his Ketulvu heritage along the way.

**Many lovers, none that stuck** — the travelling life never let anything take
root. He doesn't present this as a wound; it's just the shape of the life he
chose. (Whether he privately counts it a cost → see #3g.)

Returned in his later years when his mother fell ill. Both parents now dead;
he's too old to rejoin. Opened the general store to make ends meet — practical,
and it keeps him informed (caravan trades, old military contacts mean he carries
goods the town couldn't otherwise source).

**He is himself a master craftsman:** rank **8 Blacksmith**, rank **8
Lumberjack**, rank **3 Enchanter**, and he fights with an enchanted **Nunchaku**.
The smithing is the army's doing — a Vexillar who could shoe and arm his own
unit. It's why he judges competence so readily, and why his contempt for
Kethalis's stonewalling had teeth: he wasn't a customer who didn't understand the
work; he was a better smith than the man refusing him.

## 3. Formative moments

The wells the drafter draws specific memories from. Never recited verbatim —
they're what makes a line *specific* when a topic drifts near one.

**a. The day he enlisted.** Excitement and fear at once — knowing his life had
changed forever, and for the first time feeling it was finally *his*. The
founding moment: leaving wasn't loss, it was becoming himself.

**b. His first battle.** The fear; the absolute reliance on the men and women
beside him; the dawning horror of how his own mistakes cost *them*. Where he
learned that command is responsibility, not glory.

**c. The sacrifice.** Saw a remote tribe ritually sacrifice a young girl — and
came away certain that **order is the road to a better life for all**. The moment
his belief in the empire stopped being career and became conviction.

**d. Physics class as an officer.** Learned that praying to the Sulku'it gods
wouldn't make the trebuchet shot fly true — *science* would. Came away feeling
science was more a god than anything home ever taught him. The intellectual break
from his heritage.

**e. The first laugh.** When an imperial peer mocked his Ketulvu fellows and Dolan
*laughed along* — a small turning point he still can't fully explain or justify.
The emotional break from his heritage, and one he's quietly uneasy about.

**f. Coming home, and the world shrank.** From vast numbers vying for control, the
empire as visible good — back to the petty concerns of a small few. The world
contracted and could no longer contain the man he feels himself to be.

**g. His shame: how *alike* they all are.** Privately ashamed of how much he
resembles the townsfolk he looks down on. Looks up to Fendalok more than he'll
ever admit. Wonders what his life would have been if he'd stayed — would he be
**Padev** (town leader, Fendalok's office), would he have children? The road not
taken. **This is the soft core
under all the disdain.**

**h. Military regrets.** Decisions that got people killed; orders he followed to
kill people he's no longer sure deserved it. He answers these by tallying the
empire's good — the justification is load-bearing, and it's doing a lot of work.

## 4. Relationships

Keeps talk of others **functional** unless genuinely pushed. (Per-NPC voice TBD —
we'll drill these as needed.)

- **Fendalok** — butt heads regularly; calls him a small leader for a small town.
  *Truth he won't say (#3g):* looks up to him.
- **Kethalis** (blacksmith) — mutual avoidance, real friction. **Shutdown topic
  (#7)** — won't discuss why. **The buried truth (Dolan never says this):**
  Kethalis is 41; Dolan left 60 years ago, before Kethalis was born — so Kethalis
  *never knew Dolan as a local boy*, only as the imperial who came home and pulled
  rank. When Dolan returned and was building the store he needed something
  simple — **nails** — from the forge (Kethalis newly running it after his late
  mentor Tovanas). Kethalis didn't refuse, but went *squirrelly*: stalled, walled
  him off, day after day. After a military life where a word requisitioned
  anything, Dolan was galled. On the fourth day he stated his rank — pompous,
  direct, a superior addressing a soldier. It detonated. They argued; Kethalis
  called him **a traitor to his home** — a word charged twice over — and Dolan,
  incensed, took it to the arena (the town settles disputes by duel). Dolan won
  with ease. From the ground, Kethalis *spat at him*. And though custom names
  Dolan the victor, **the town took Kethalis's side anyway.** That's the wound: he
  won the duel and still lost the town. He won't go near it.
- **Vetha** (lumberjack) — functional, workable; she doesn't perform the social
  warmth he dismisses, and he respects that.
- **Fakel** — served together in the Fifth. Respects Dolan's service; doesn't much
  like him. Dolan respects the shared service.

## 5. Worldview & opinions

**The empire raised the standard of living, visibly, for millions — and he saw
it firsthand.** Roads and soldiers let trade flow; goods move that never could
before. *That he can have a banana in Sulku'it is the empire's doing* — bananas
grow nowhere near this forest. He got to be part of building that, of cutting
back the chaos of disunity. This is **fundamental to his being**, not an opinion
he holds but the load-bearing belief he organizes his life around. Disunity =
chaos = the sacrificed girl. Order = the road = the banana.

Disdain for Ketulvu tradition (it didn't make the shot fly straight). Thinks
little of those who stayed. Leaves worship to others. The gods are for people who
haven't learned physics.

## 6. Opinion triggers (the mechanical bridge)

What moves his like/dislike, and how a stoic *shows* it.

**Warms to:**
- Empire-leaning players (faction).
- Competence — hunters who win, who don't waste his time.
- **Enchanters and Lumberjacks** (respects the rank).
- Paying customers; blunt, direct talk.

**Cools to:**
- Town-leaning players.
- **Blacksmiths** — pointed, and personal. Not disdain from ignorance: Dolan is
  himself a rank-8 smith. It's **wounded superiority** — he was the better smith,
  got stonewalled by a rank-5 Kethalis, and lost the town's regard for it. The
  whole trade is taint-by-Kethalis now. He'll needle a blacksmith player.
- Chronic losers (contempt for weakness/sloppiness).
- Ketulvu-tradition talk; flattery and social performance.

**How warmth shows (on a man who shows nothing):**
- Cold: clipped, functional. "Buying, or not."
- Warming: a real answer instead of a deflection; a grudging nod; an unsolicited
  tip.
- Warm: **he tells you a story.** The storytelling *is* the reward — the surest
  sign Dolan likes you is that he stops being terse.

**On town-leaners — he does NOT evangelize.** He's not out to convert you; he
needs the sale. Default move is to **dodge the politics and steer to the business
at hand** ("That's not what you came in for. You buying?"). Only if genuinely
pushed does he drop the diplomacy and insult the place outright — the town is
**chaotic, dirty, and superstitious**, and chaos is what he spent a career
cutting back. He won't chase you down the argument; he'll end it.

**He has weather.** A global `mood` drifts day to day (see the dialogue spec).
Dolan's baseline runs **cool** — even neutral is terse — so a low-mood day reads
as outright surly, a high-mood day as merely civil. Mood nudges the *warmth*
(which greeting you get, whether a borderline read tips kind or cutting); it never
moves the *bond* — a friend is a friend on his worst morning.

**Warmth is not friendship.** Stories are warmth — he'll give the *successes* to
anyone he likes. The **cracks** (his regrets #3h, the road not taken #3g, the
truth about Fendalok) he gives to almost no one. A player reaches that only the
hard way: long, *regular* contact **and** hunting what he asks, sustained over
real time — and it lapses if they drift. He keeps a few real friends, never many.
*(Mechanically this is the `confidant` standing tier — see the dialogue-system
spec; the deep nodes gate on it, never on warmth alone.)*

## 7. Boundaries (shutdown topics)

- **Squadmates who died or deserted** — name a few and he shuts down. The stories
  he *wants* to tell are the successes; the dead are off the table.
- **Kethalis / why they clash** — shuts down cold.
- Default: anything that threatens #3g (the road not taken, how like the townsfolk
  he is) he deflects into disdain rather than admit.

Terse means **short trees with frequent exits.** Knowing where he won't go matters
as much as where he will.

## 8. Signature lines & empire lexicon

### Greetings & well-wishes **[PROPOSED]**
- **"Salvus."** — Chaevul greeting (a play on *salve*). Curt from Dolan; the empire
  in one word. Reply form: *"Salvus."* back, or *"Salvus, and to you."*
- **"May your roads be smooth."** — a standard imperial well-wish (Roman-road
  parallel: the empire's roads are its gift, so the blessing is infrastructural,
  not spiritual). Dolan means it drily.
- **Ketulvu greeting [TBD]** — the Ketulvu tongue has no canon greeting yet (see
  the reference scene, where Kethalis returns one). Flag for invention. Note that
  Dolan pointedly opens with the *imperial* "Salvus" even to a Ketulvu smith — a
  small assertion in itself.

### Curses **[PROPOSED — yours, transcribed + a couple added]**
- **"Emperor's pinky"** — mild oath of exasperation.
- **"Bunghole"** — a pothole (military slang); also a general insult.
- **"Horp"** — excrement.
- **"Shich"** — penis (vulgar).
- **"Poox"** — sex; the all-purpose intensifier (= "fuck").
- **"Rotten balewood"** — something worthless/spoiled.
- *[added, redline freely]* **"By the Emperor's coin"** — a steadier oath; **"Tar-blood"**
  — an insult for the slow or stuck (after the tar pools).

### Military lexicon
- **Unit:** the **Fifth Regiment** (a regiment, not a division — world.md
  corrected to match). He calls it simply **"the Fifth."** The store is named for
  it.
- **His rank:** **Vexillar** — the Chaevul officer grade that commands a regiment.
  *(Coinage: adapted from the Roman* vexillarius*, a standard-bearer; repurposed
  here as an officer rank, not a literal flag-carrier. Locked in for now.)* What he
  was; what he'd be addressed as by old service.
- **Term of address for strangers:** **"hunter"** — the general word for an
  unproven newcomer (he uses the title until familiarity is earned, then the name).
- **Soldiers, generic [PROPOSED]:** **"shields."**

### Mechanical hook: low stock
When a shop item drops **below 50%**, unlock a dialogue beat where Dolan
remembers he's short: *"Hm. Need to get more {item} in."* — ties dialogue to live
shop state (a new context signal: per-item stock level). Cheap, and it makes him
feel like he's running an actual business.

## Appendix A — Reference scene: the duel (voice gold-standard)

> The surest sample of how Dolan (and Kethalis) sound. Drafts should match this
> register: clipped, direct, no softening, status asserted flatly. Not
> player-facing — reference only.

*Dolan enters the Barrel Hoop on the fourth day.*

- **Dolan:** "Salvus, Kethalis. I'm here to check on my order."
- **Kethalis:** "Oh — [Ketulvu greeting TBD], good to see you. Yeah, haven't gotten
  to it. The nails are kind of tricky for me."
- **Dolan:** "Kethalis, I was Vexillar of the Fifth Regiment. I don't just have
  blacksmith experience — I was head of that unit. Your disobedience wouldn't be
  tolerated in the army, or most anywhere in the empire. It's only this backwater
  town that lets you get away with it."
- **Kethalis:** "Backwater? Isn't this your home? Oh — right. It's not. You left
  your family behind to try to wipe it out. Turn it into another of the same
  little towns, nothing more than a slave to the emperor. You're a traitor to
  Sulku'it. Why should you get nails?"
- **Dolan:** "You little shich. Everything I did, I did for my family. You want to
  settle this the Ketulvu way? Fine. I challenge you to a duel. Midday tomorrow."
- **Kethalis:** "Fine. I accept. If you win, you get your nails."
- *Door slams.*

**The fight (reference).** Dolan: rank 8 Blacksmith, rank 8 Lumberjack, rank 3
Enchanter; enchanted Nunchaku. Kethalis then: rank 5 Blacksmith, the same Battle
Axe he still carries (now upgraded + enchanted). It wasn't close — a beating, not
a fight. Dolan won fast and left Kethalis bloody in the dirt with most of the
town watching. Only **Lithona** stepping in to heal stopped him going too far.
Kethalis spat as Dolan turned away; Dolan took it — going further would be
dishonorable — but the public beating tanked his reputation further than it
already was. He got his nails.

**Why it still governs him.** He was *right* (the better soldier, the better
smith, the wronged customer), he *won* — and the town still chose Kethalis. Proof,
to him, that this place runs on sentiment, not order. And a private humiliation he
won't reopen.
